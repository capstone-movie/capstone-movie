Jessica Smith (Casual Anime Fan)

Age: 25

Gender: Female

Profession

  • Jessica is a normal person with a casual 9-5 job doing social media marketing at Walmart Corporation. When her friends and co-worker talks about the new anime they are watching, she starts asking for recommendations, in responds they are like if you like X anime, you'll love Y anime. She's like, omg i can't wait to check it out when I get home. She ain't a hardcore fan... yet...

Technology

  • Jessica is very familiar with websites instagram, TikTok, netflix and facebook. She's not afraid of clicking on ads and getting redirected to new interesting websites.
  • Uses the new iPhone with the latest OS
  • Uses a Macbook pro to watch Netflix

Attitude & Behaviors

  • Friendly and talkative, but nervous when participating in conversations where she isn't familiar with the topic.
  • Enjoys reading the overview of a show before watching it.
  • Takes into account the rating of a show when deciding what to watch.
  • Enjoys doom scrolling on TikTok.
  • Enjoys binging shows on Netflix.
  • Enjoys liking post on Facebook and Instagram.

Frustrations

  • She doesn't always have something to contribute when her co-workers talks about the latest anime they are watching.
  • She doesn't like to make an account on a website to access content, unless if she really likes the website and community.

Goals

  • To find new interesting anime to watch, and talking points to discuss with her co-workers and friends.

User Story

  • As a user, I want to get recommendations about the latest and greatest anime, especially shows that are related to what I've watched and enjoyed. Especially if it gives me a talking point to discuss with my friends and co-workers.

Use Case

  • Jessica wants anime recommendations based on her personal preferences.

Pre-condition

  • Jessica watched and enjoyed anime in the past, and is open to new recommendations.
  • She doesn't have an account for our website.
  • She's logged into Netflix, and Crunchy Roll, websites where she can legally watch anime.

Post-condition

  • She receives recommendations based on her personal preferences.
  • She created an account for this website.
  • She has a curated list of her favorite anime.
  • She has a curated list of anime she is planning to watch.
  • She gains talking point when discussing anime with her friends and co-workers.
  • She continues to use this website, and add to her list, and get better recommendations.

Interaction Flow

  • User: Jessica opens up the anime recommendations website.
  • Website: Search Bar, middle of screen, Anime Thumbnails surrounding it
  • User: Clicks on thumbnail that catches her eyes.
  • Website: Auto-fills search bar then redirects her to the page for the selected anime.
  • User: She spots a recommendation section that recommends similar anime based on the current anime page.
    She clicks on a recommended anime.
  • Website: Website brings her to the new page.
  • User: She spots a "Save for later" button.
    She clicks that button
    • Website: Website prompts her to enter her email account to create a "watch later" list. (just email, no passwords)
  • User: She enters her email.
  • Website: Website sends a "Magic link", and redirect to her personal dashboard. (linked to that email)
    Dashboard contains 3 sections of list, "Favorite", "Watch Later", and "Recommendations".
  • User: She clicks on the first item in the "Recommendations" list
  • Website: Website redirects her to the specific anime page.
    On the page is a watch now button, linking to Netflix, CrunchyRoll, etc
  • User: She presses the watch now button.
  • Website: Website opens a new tab, taking her to where she can watch the anime.
  • User: After binging the series, she returns to the website,
  • Website: Nothing changes because website opened up the link in a new tab.
  • User: She clicks the "Add to favorite" button.
  • Website: The anime thumbnails animate towards the "recommendations" tab on the nav bar
  • User: She clicks the "recommendations" button links.
  • Website: Website brings her to her personalize dashboard. The added anime thumbnails fly into the Favorite list, More recommendations anime thumbnails starts to populate the recommendations list
  • User: She clicks on another recommendations that catches her eyes.
  • Website: Website redirects her to that page.
  • User: She doesn't find this one interesting, so she presses the "Hide"
  • Website: Website no longer shows that anime in future search results / recommendations. (There's a list that shows all "hidden" titles in the settings, and user can just remove titles from that list to have them show up again.

Conclusion

  • She can get recommendations based on her favorite anime, maintain a to-watch list, keep track of her favorites, and curate her search result by hiding anime that she doesn't care for.
